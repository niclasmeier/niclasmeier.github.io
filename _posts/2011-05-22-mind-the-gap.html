It's sunday and I am fed up, sitting lazy behind my computer and taking a review of the last week. I attended two conferences this week so I am full of new ideas (don't know if they are good yet) and wan't to take a closer look on so many topics.

The first conference was the next conference</a> in Berlin which was initiated some years a go by my former employer SinnerSchrader</a>. So the focus of the next conference</a> was mixture of brand, media, entrepreneurship and technology. Due to the fact that I meet a couple of former colleagues - some of them still working for SinnerSchrader</a>.

The second conference was the Developer Conference Hamburg 2011</a> which had a sole technology focus.I heard couple of very good talks on both conferences. My highlight was the the keynote of Werner Vogels</a> the CTO of Amazon which spoke about the analysis of big dataset which are regularly produced by modern IT infrastructures. If you generate a couple of gigabytes of data every day you should get the information out of them.

Another highlight of both conferences where the Couch DB</a> talks. Both held by Jan Lehnhardt</a>, one of the Couch DB</a> core commiters who works for Couchbase</a>. Jan can talk very fast in his talks, especially when he had some coffee :-). But there is no bull*** and he definitely knows what he is talking about. Because I attended the next</a> and the devcon</a> I was able to get a 10.000m overview and a 100m close look on the Couch DB</a>. I am very fascinated by this topic, but I have at the moment no idea where to use it.

A dilemma what Couch DB</a> shares with node.js</a>. I heard of node</a> on both conference too. On the next node.js</a> was presented by Isaac Z. Schlueter</a>. The topic was how node.js</a> performs on DIRT (d</u>ata i</u>ntensive r</u>eal t</u>ime) applications. At devcon</a> node.js</a> was presented by Felix Geisendörfer</a> which gave a more wide range overview on the capabilites of node.js</a>. Unfortunately my current employer</a> does at the moment some kind of "boring" ;-) e-commerce stuff, so no real use for node here. Some SinnerSchrader</a> guys had a very innovative idea using node building the The World’s First Digital Foosball Table</a> - nice one!

But the most influencing talks where from Prof. Dr. Friedrich Esser</a> from the HAW Hamburg</a> about Scala</a>. The talk and the workshops where full of snappy remarks on Java</a> programming language. I am doing Java for over ten years now. And I am confident that I am pretty good at Java</a> and it was my favorite language the last ten years. But in the recent time I am feeling bored by Java</a> and it's very tight restrictions. We'll see where the journey goes with this Scala</a> stuff.

On the other hand side there were also some talks - which we presented brilliantly - that where about stuff that I am doing for a couple of year now. One of these was the "Abstract monitoring" talk from Dr. Johannes Mainusch</a> from Xing. Very nice talk, I agree 100% but I was also doing this since 2005. Good to see that other developers are cooking with water too ;-)

But the last week reminded me of the gap what we have at the moment and what I am supposed to close as soon as possible. But now I am armed with some new ideas ...
